# Meta-Review and Editorial Decision
## Multilocus Species-Tree Reconstruction Reveals Deep Lineage Structure in East African Dwarf Galagos (*Paragalago*)

---

## Executive Summary

This manuscript presents a multilocus phylogenetic analysis of East African dwarf galagos (*Paragalago*) using StarBEAST3, recovering four well-supported lineages and providing the first molecular data from Malawi populations. All three reviewers recognize the study's significant contribution to galago systematics and East African biogeography, particularly the inclusion of previously unsampled Malawi populations. However, all reviewers identify substantial methodological, analytical, and presentation issues that must be addressed before publication. The consensus recommendation is **Accept with Major Revisions**.

## Areas of Consensus Among Reviewers

### 1. Major Contribution: First Malawi Molecular Data

**All three reviewers unanimously agree** that the inclusion of Malawi *Paragalago* samples represents the study's most significant contribution, filling a critical 30+ year gap since Courtenay & Bearder's (1989) acoustic surveys. This alone justifies publication and provides essential baseline data for taxonomy, conservation, and biogeography.

### 2. Sound Core Phylogenetic Framework

**All reviewers concur** that the phylogenetic analysis is technically sound, employing appropriate modern methods (StarBEAST3) and yielding robust results with high posterior support (PP ≥ 0.9998). The four-lineage structure (*P. z. zanzibaricus*, *P. z. udzungwensis*, *P. rondoensis*, Malawi lineage) is well-supported and represents a reliable foundation for future work.

### 3. Critical Limitation: Lack of Absolute Time Calibration

**All three reviewers identify** the absence of absolute time calibration as a major limitation that severely constrains biogeographic interpretation. Without temporal framework:
- Cannot test whether divergences correspond to Pleistocene climate cycles
- Cannot compare to paleoclimate events or dated phylogenies of other taxa
- Cannot evaluate refugial persistence times
- Biogeographic discussion remains qualitative rather than quantitative

**Consensus recommendation**: Implementing relaxed-clock models with fossil or biogeographic calibrations is **essential** for revision.

### 4. Incomplete Taxonomic Sampling

**All reviewers note** that missing key lineages (*P. cocos*, *P. orinus*, *P. granti* type locality) limits the comprehensiveness of the phylogeny and means relationships may change with denser sampling. While this doesn't invalidate current findings, it should be explicitly acknowledged as a limitation.

### 5. Methodological Gaps

**Reviewers 1 and 2 particularly emphasize**:
- **No introgression testing**: MSC assumes no gene flow, but galagos may hybridize in contact zones
- **No validation across methods**: Only StarBEAST3 used; no comparison to ASTRAL, concatenation, or BPP
- **No formal species delimitation**: High PP suggestive but objective tests (BPP, STACEY) would strengthen taxonomic conclusions
- **Missing MCMC diagnostics**: ESS values, trace plots, and convergence assessment not reported

### 6. Incomplete Data Presentation

**Reviewers 2 and 3 particularly emphasize**:
- **No sample information table**: Localities, collection dates, sample sizes per lineage not provided
- **No sequence characteristics**: Alignment lengths, variable sites, missing data not reported
- **No genetic distance matrices**: Cannot quantitatively assess divergence levels
- **Vague quantitative statements**: "~3% of total tree height" instead of specific values

### 7. Underdeveloped Broader Implications

**Reviewer 3 particularly emphasizes**, with support from Reviewers 1 and 2:
- **Insufficient positioning**: Doesn't adequately compare to previous work (Pozzi et al. 2020, Courtenay & Bearder 1989)
- **General conservation recommendations**: Lack specific actions, IUCN assessments, or priority populations
- **Limited comparative biogeography**: No systematic comparison to other East African taxa
- **Taxonomic implications not fully developed**: Doesn't thoroughly address species delimitation criteria

## Areas of Disagreement Among Reviewers

### 1. Severity of Locus Sampling Limitation

**Reviewer 1** (Methods Specialist) views the six-locus dataset as a significant limitation, noting that "many independent loci" are recommended for robust MSC inference and that nuclear sampling is particularly limited (only 3 nuclear loci, 2 from same gene).

**Reviewers 2 and 3** view the locus sampling as adequate though not optimal, noting it's competitive with recent galago studies.

**Resolution**: While six loci are acceptable for this dataset size, the authors should acknowledge this limitation and note that additional independent nuclear loci would strengthen confidence in results. This is a moderate rather than critical weakness.

### 2. Soundness Ratings

**Reviewer 1** gives soundness 3/5, emphasizing methodological limitations (no time calibration, no introgression testing, no validation).

**Reviewer 3** gives soundness 4/5, viewing the core phylogenetic framework as sound despite analytical gaps.

**Reviewer 2** gives soundness 3/5, focusing on experimental design issues (sample sizes, data quality reporting).

**Resolution**: The core phylogenetic analysis is sound (high PP, appropriate methods), but additional analyses (time calibration, introgression testing, validation) are needed to strengthen inferences. Soundness rating of 3-4/5 is appropriate depending on whether one emphasizes what's present (robust topology) or what's missing (additional analyses).

### 3. Presentation Quality

**Reviewer 1** gives presentation 4/5, viewing writing as generally good but methods needing more detail.

**Reviewer 2** gives presentation 3/5, emphasizing major deficiencies in data presentation and methods description.

**Reviewer 3** gives presentation 4/5, viewing writing as clear but broader implications underdeveloped.

**Resolution**: The manuscript is clearly written with good organization, but Methods and Results sections need substantial expansion with comprehensive data tables and quantitative details. Presentation rating of 3-4/5 is appropriate.

### 4. Priority of Revisions

**Reviewer 1** prioritizes: (1) time calibration, (2) introgression testing, (3) formal species delimitation, (4) method comparison.

**Reviewer 2** prioritizes: (1) comprehensive data tables, (2) time calibration, (3) population genetic analyses, (4) expanded sampling.

**Reviewer 3** prioritizes: (1) positioning relative to prior work, (2) broader implications, (3) comprehensive data tables, (4) explicit hypotheses.

**Resolution**: All priorities are valid. The essential revisions for acceptance are: (1) time calibration, (2) comprehensive data tables, (3) introgression testing, (4) improved positioning and broader implications. Other recommendations are highly encouraged but not absolutely required.

## Editorial Assessment

### Strengths of the Manuscript

1. **Fills critical knowledge gap**: First molecular data from Malawi *Paragalago* populations after 30+ years
2. **Robust phylogenetic framework**: High statistical support (PP ≥ 0.9998) for all nodes
3. **Appropriate modern methods**: StarBEAST3 well-suited for this dataset
4. **Clear biogeographic implications**: Identifies distinct refugial systems (Rondo, Malawi)
5. **Conservation relevance**: Provides framework for ESU definition and prioritization
6. **Clear writing**: Well-organized and accessible to broad audience
7. **Taxonomic conservatism**: Appropriately cautious about naming Malawi lineage

### Critical Weaknesses Requiring Revision

1. **Lack of absolute time calibration** (all reviewers): Essential for biogeographic interpretation
2. **No introgression testing** (Reviewers 1, 2): Potential MSC model violation unaddressed
3. **Incomplete data presentation** (Reviewers 2, 3): Missing sample tables, sequence characteristics, genetic distances
4. **Insufficient MCMC diagnostics** (Reviewers 1, 2): ESS values, convergence assessment not reported
5. **Underdeveloped broader implications** (Reviewer 3): Conservation, comparative biogeography need expansion
6. **Insufficient positioning** (Reviewer 3): Comparison to previous work inadequate

### Moderate Weaknesses (Highly Recommended to Address)

1. **No formal species delimitation**: BPP or STACEY would strengthen taxonomic conclusions
2. **No validation across methods**: ASTRAL or concatenation comparison would assess robustness
3. **Modest locus sampling**: Additional independent nuclear loci would improve power
4. **Incomplete taxonomic sampling**: Missing *P. cocos*, *P. orinus*, type localities limits comprehensiveness
5. **No population genetic analyses**: Diversity, demographics, Ne estimates would inform conservation
6. **No integrative taxonomy**: Acoustic and morphological data would provide independent validation

### Minor Weaknesses (Recommended but Not Required)

1. **Introduction lacks explicit hypotheses**: Would improve clarity of research questions
2. **Discussion structure**: Subheadings would enhance organization
3. **Limitations section absent**: Should explicitly discuss constraints
4. **Biogeographic modeling**: Formal DEC/DIVA analysis would strengthen inferences
5. **Comparative phylogeography**: Systematic comparison to other East African taxa

## Required Revisions for Acceptance

### Essential Revisions (Must be addressed)

1. **Implement absolute time calibration**
   - Use fossil calibrations (Galagidae crown age) or biogeographic priors (Zanzibar island formation ~5 Ma)
   - Apply relaxed-clock models in StarBEAST3
   - Report divergence time estimates with 95% HPD intervals
   - Discuss temporal framework in relation to Pleistocene climate cycles
   - **Justification**: All three reviewers identify this as critical; without temporal framework, biogeographic interpretation is severely limited

2. **Test for introgression/gene flow**
   - Conduct ABBA-BABA tests (D-statistics) to detect gene flow
   - If introgression detected, discuss implications for species boundaries and MSC assumptions
   - Consider phylogenetic networks or MSci models if significant gene flow found
   - **Justification**: Reviewers 1 and 2 emphasize this as essential to validate MSC assumptions

3. **Add comprehensive data tables**
   - **Sample information table**: Sample ID, lineage, locality with coordinates, collection date, tissue type, voucher number, GenBank accessions
   - **Sequence characteristics table**: Locus, alignment length, variable sites, parsimony-informative sites, GC content, missing data
   - **Genetic distance matrix**: Pairwise uncorrected and model-corrected distances within and between lineages
   - **Justification**: Reviewers 2 and 3 emphasize this as essential for reproducibility and quantitative assessment

4. **Report MCMC diagnostics thoroughly**
   - State run length, burn-in percentage, sampling frequency
   - Report ESS values for tree topology and key parameters (table or supplementary)
   - Include trace plots in supplementary materials
   - Describe convergence assessment procedures
   - **Justification**: Reviewers 1 and 2 identify this as essential for validating Bayesian inference

5. **Expand Discussion of broader implications**
   - Add dedicated subsection on **Conservation Implications** with specific recommendations for each lineage
   - Add dedicated subsection on **Comparative Biogeography** comparing to other East African taxa
   - Strengthen positioning relative to previous work (explicit comparison to Pozzi et al. 2020, Courtenay & Bearder 1989)
   - Add **Limitations and Future Directions** subsection
   - **Justification**: Reviewer 3 emphasizes this as essential; Reviewers 1 and 2 support

6. **Expand Methods section**
   - Add detailed molecular protocols (DNA extraction, PCR primers, sequencing)
   - Specify BEAST parameters (priors, substitution models, operators)
   - Describe model selection procedures
   - Add ethics approval and permit statements
   - **Justification**: Reviewers 2 and 3 emphasize insufficient detail for replication

### Highly Recommended Revisions (Strongly encouraged)

1. **Perform formal species delimitation analyses**
   - Use BPP or STACEY to objectively test species boundaries
   - Compare molecular delimitation to morphological/acoustic species concepts
   - **Justification**: Reviewer 1 emphasizes this; would strengthen taxonomic conclusions

2. **Compare to alternative phylogenetic methods**
   - Run ASTRAL (summary coalescent) for validation
   - Compare to concatenated ML/Bayesian analyses
   - Discuss concordance/discordance and implications
   - **Justification**: Reviewer 1 emphasizes this; would assess robustness to methodological choices

3. **Add population genetic analyses**
   - Calculate genetic diversity (π, haplotype diversity) for each lineage
   - Infer demographic history using extended Bayesian skyline plots
   - Extract Ne estimates from StarBEAST3 or calculate separately
   - **Justification**: Reviewer 2 emphasizes this; would inform conservation priorities

4. **Add explicit hypotheses to Introduction**
   - State testable predictions about phylogenetic relationships
   - Articulate biogeographic scenarios being tested
   - Specify taxonomic expectations
   - **Justification**: Reviewer 3 emphasizes this; would improve clarity

5. **Create comparative phylogeographic framework**
   - Compile data from other East African taxa (chameleons, amphibians, birds)
   - Create table comparing branching order, divergences, phylogeographic breaks
   - Discuss congruence/discordance and implications
   - **Justification**: All reviewers note this would strengthen biogeographic interpretation

### Recommended Revisions (Encouraged but not required for acceptance)

1. Expand taxonomic sampling (obtain *P. cocos*, *P. orinus*, *P. granti* type locality samples)
2. Integrate acoustic and morphological data if available
3. Perform formal biogeographic modeling (DEC, DIVA, BioGeoBEARS)
4. Add graphical abstract for broad accessibility
5. Improve figure quality and legends
6. Prepare comprehensive supplementary materials

## Specific Responses to Reviewer Questions

Several reviewers posed specific questions that authors should address in revision:

### Methodological Questions (Reviewer 1)
- Why was absolute time calibration not performed? Are fossil or biogeographic calibrations unavailable?
- Have you tested for gene flow between lineages?
- Do individual gene trees recover the same topology as the species tree?
- What substitution models were selected for each locus?
- What were the ESS values and MCMC run parameters?

### Data Questions (Reviewer 2)
- How many individuals represent each lineage?
- Can you provide precise localities with coordinates?
- Where specifically in Malawi were samples collected?
- What are the alignment lengths and numbers of variable sites per locus?
- What percentage of data is missing per individual/locus?
- What are the pairwise genetic distances within and between lineages?

### Interpretation Questions (Reviewer 3)
- What specific hypotheses were you testing?
- How do results compare to Pozzi et al. (2020)?
- Do genetic lineages correspond to acoustic types from Courtenay & Bearder (1989)?
- Do you recommend elevating subspecies to species status?
- What are current IUCN statuses and do results suggest reassessments?

**Authors should address these questions explicitly in the revised manuscript or in a response letter.**

## Editorial Decision: ACCEPT WITH MAJOR REVISIONS

### Justification

This manuscript makes a significant contribution to galago systematics and East African biogeography by providing the first molecular data from Malawi *Paragalago* populations and establishing a robust phylogenetic framework. All three reviewers recognize the importance of this work and recommend publication pending substantial revisions.

The core phylogenetic findings are sound and well-supported (PP ≥ 0.9998), and the use of modern multispecies coalescent methods is appropriate. The identification of four distinct lineages with clear biogeographic implications provides an important foundation for taxonomy, conservation, and understanding East African forest refugia dynamics.

However, all three reviewers identify critical limitations that must be addressed:
1. **Lack of absolute time calibration** severely constrains biogeographic interpretation
2. **No introgression testing** leaves potential model violations unaddressed
3. **Incomplete data presentation** reduces reproducibility and quantitative assessment
4. **Underdeveloped broader implications** limit impact beyond galago specialists

These are addressable through revision and do not require new data collection (though expanded sampling would be beneficial for future work).

### Conditions for Acceptance

The manuscript will be acceptable for publication if the authors address:

**Essential revisions** (all must be completed):
1. Implement absolute time calibration with relaxed-clock models
2. Test for introgression using ABBA-BABA or similar methods
3. Add comprehensive data tables (samples, sequences, genetic distances)
4. Report MCMC diagnostics thoroughly (ESS, convergence)
5. Expand Discussion of broader implications (conservation, comparative biogeography, positioning)
6. Expand Methods with detailed protocols and parameters

**Highly recommended revisions** (at least 2-3 should be addressed):
1. Perform formal species delimitation (BPP or STACEY)
2. Compare to alternative methods (ASTRAL, concatenation)
3. Add population genetic analyses (diversity, demographics, Ne)
4. Add explicit hypotheses to Introduction
5. Create comparative phylogeographic framework

### Timeline

Given the scope of required revisions, authors should be given **3-4 months** to complete revisions. The revised manuscript will be sent back to all three reviewers for assessment of whether revisions adequately address concerns.

### Target Journal Recommendations

**With successful major revisions**, this manuscript would be suitable for:
- **Tier 1 (preferred)**: *Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution*, *Journal of Biogeography*, *BMC Evolutionary Biology*, *Systematic Biology*
- **Tier 2 (acceptable)**: *Folia Primatologica*, *International Journal of Primatology*, *Primate Conservation*

The manuscript has the potential to be published in a top-tier systematic or biogeographic journal if revisions are thorough and address all essential concerns.

## Final Recommendation

**ACCEPT WITH MAJOR REVISIONS**

This manuscript addresses an important gap in galago systematics and East African biogeography. The inclusion of Malawi populations and establishment of a robust phylogenetic framework represent significant contributions that justify publication. However, critical methodological and presentation issues must be addressed before the work can reach its full potential.

The required revisions are substantial but achievable within 3-4 months and do not require new data collection. With thorough revisions addressing the essential concerns raised by all three reviewers, this manuscript will be suitable for publication in a high-quality systematic or biogeographic journal and will make an important contribution to understanding primate evolution and East African biodiversity.

**Authors are encouraged to address all essential revisions and as many highly recommended revisions as possible to maximize the impact and significance of this important work.**
