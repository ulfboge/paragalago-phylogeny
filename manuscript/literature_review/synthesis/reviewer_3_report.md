# Reviewer 3 Report: Clarity, Positioning & Broader Impact Specialist

## Summary

This manuscript presents a multilocus phylogenetic analysis of East African dwarf galagos (*Paragalago*) using StarBEAST3 and six genetic markers from 24 individuals. The authors recover four well-supported lineages: *P. zanzibaricus zanzibaricus* (Zanzibar), *P. z. udzungwensis* (mainland Tanzania), *P. rondoensis* (Rondo Plateau), and a novel Malawi lineage. The phylogeny reveals deep lineage structure with *P. rondoensis* as the basal split, followed by the Malawi lineage, and recent Zanzibar-Udzungwa divergence. The study fills a critical gap by providing first molecular data from Malawi and contributes to understanding East African refugia dynamics. While well-written and making important contributions, clarity could be improved in several areas, positioning relative to prior work needs strengthening, and broader implications deserve more thorough treatment.

## Soundness: 4/5

The phylogenetic analysis is technically sound using appropriate modern methods (StarBEAST3), yielding robust results with high posterior support (PP ≥ 0.9998). The multilocus approach combining mitochondrial and nuclear markers follows established best practices. Main conclusions about lineage structure and biogeographic patterns are well-supported.

**Strengths:**
- Appropriate use of multispecies coalescent framework
- High statistical support for all nodes
- Proper outgroup selection and geographic sampling
- Methodological choices well-justified

**Limitations:**
- Lack of absolute time calibration limits biogeographic interpretation
- No formal species delimitation analyses
- Missing validation through alternative methods
- No introgression testing
- Modest locus sampling (6 loci) adequate but not optimal

## Presentation: 4/5

The manuscript is generally well-written with clear structure and appropriate technical language. Abstract effectively summarizes the work, and main findings are presented clearly.

**Strengths:**
- Clear, informative abstract
- Logical organization following standard structure
- Effective introduction providing good context
- Appropriate technical terminology
- Relevant literature appropriately cited

**Major deficiencies:**

1. **Introduction lacks explicit hypotheses**: Doesn't articulate specific testable predictions about phylogenetic relationships, biogeographic scenarios, or taxonomic expectations.

2. **Methods section lacks essential details**: Missing sample information table, molecular protocols, sequence characteristics, and complete BEAST parameters.

3. **Results lack quantitative detail**: Vague statements like "~3% of total tree height" instead of specific values; no genetic distance matrices; MCMC diagnostics not reported.

4. **Insufficient positioning relative to prior work**: Doesn't adequately compare to Pozzi et al. (2020), Courtenay & Bearder (1989), or articulate what's novel vs. confirmatory.

5. **Broader implications underdeveloped**: Conservation recommendations too general; comparative biogeography not systematic; taxonomic implications not thoroughly discussed.

6. **Supplementary materials not mentioned**: No indication of data availability, supplementary files, or repository deposition.

**Recommendations:**
- Add comprehensive sample and sequence tables
- Expand Methods with detailed protocols
- Make Results more quantitative
- Strengthen Discussion positioning and broader implications
- Include supplementary materials and data availability statements

## Suggestions

1. **Strengthen Introduction with explicit hypotheses**: Add research questions, phylogenetic predictions, biogeographic scenarios, and taxonomic expectations.

2. **Add explicit comparisons to previous work**: Create structured comparisons showing concordant, novel, and discordant findings vs. Pozzi et al. (2020) and others.

3. **Expand Discussion with dedicated subsections**:
   - Conservation Implications (specific recommendations per lineage)
   - Comparative Biogeography (systematic comparison to other taxa)
   - Refugial Dynamics (implications for forest persistence)
   - Cryptic Diversity Discovery (relevance for other primates)
   - Taxonomic Framework (species delimitation criteria)

5. **Add "Taxonomic Implications" subsection**: Address species delimitation criteria, status of recognized taxa, Malawi nomenclature, integration with other data, nomenclatural priorities.

6. **Expand Methods substantially**: Add sample information table, detailed molecular protocols, sequence characteristics table, complete BEAST parameters, convergence assessment.

7. **Make Results more quantitative**: Add genetic distance matrix, specific branch lengths, sample sizes, MCMC diagnostics, gene-tree concordance.

8. **Add "Limitations and Future Directions" subsection**: Discuss incomplete sampling, modest loci, lack of calibration, single-data-type approach, and how results might change.

9. **Improve figure quality**: Ensure clear phylogenetic tree, geographic map, comprehensive legends. Consider graphical abstract.

10. **Add comprehensive supplementary materials**: Extended methods, locality data, alignments, trees, BEAST files, MCMC diagnostics, additional analyses.

11. **Add Data Availability section**: Specify GenBank accessions, Dryad DOI, TreeBASE study number, GitHub repository.

12. **Strengthen conservation recommendations**: Add IUCN assessments, priority populations, threat analysis, management actions, monitoring programs.

13. **Improve accessibility for non-specialists**: Add plain-language summary, glossary if needed, broader framing of relevance.
